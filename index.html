Hi, I'm Matt


My career has a theme: bringing new entertainment concepts to life


Developing the idea, refining the details, raising the capital, building an A+ team...
Then going to market... seeing smiles on the faces of millions of real people. So fun.

I've been fortunate to spend my career developing new location-based entertainment categories and building winning business models around them.

From developing and opening the first escape-room style concept in the world over 20 years ago, to designing & building some of your favorite attractions and exhibits at your favorite theme park and museum, to serving dozens of clients and many millions of guests, now leading the way in the emerging Challenge Room entertainment space.

Fun Stuff

In addition to my companies, I've been fortunate to work with quality people and organizations.

  • MIT: Product Design

    For 18 years, Matt has been on the MIT Faculty, as part of the teaching team for 2.009: The Product Design Process at the Massachuetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Matt's alma mater. This is the capstone class of the mechanical engineering major, as students work in teams to conceptualize a consumer product and build a fully-functional alpha prototype in 3 short months.

    I've also helped teach 2.007, the famous robot competition class. Fun, fun.

  • Act3

    In launching LEVEL99, I've had the great fortune to work with Ron Shaich, founder, chairman, and CEO of Panera Bread, chairman of CAVA, and the force behind many other successful restaurants.

    Ron's investment firm Act3 partners with consumer facing brands to help them build dominant positions in the catgories of the future.

  • Awards

    My teams have received three THEA Awards (the Oscars of themed entertainment) for work on Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge, the International Spy Museum, and Level99

    I'm also pretty proud of Level99 winning "Best Pizza in Boston" from Boston Magazine!

  • Patents

    I've been awarded several US patents relating to the configuration of digital avatars, game rooms that situationally adapt, and specific game designs.

    Others pending, stay tuned.

    And if you're a competitor, best to stay clear of our ideas and do your own thing. We're friendly, but we have expensive lawyers. :)
